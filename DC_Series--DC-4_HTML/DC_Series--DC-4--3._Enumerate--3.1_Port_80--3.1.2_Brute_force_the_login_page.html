3.1.2 Brute force the login page


a) Brute force the login page.

Make sure Burp’s “intercept” is on.



b) Go to your Browser and introduce a random Username and Password.



In this case I've introduced “user” and “1234”.

Your HTTP request will be captured by Burp and will be waiting for your action. You’ll be able to see what you’ve put for the username and password, as shown below.



Right click and send the request to Intruder. Drop the request and turn off the intercept.

c) Click on “Intruder” tab and go to the “Positions” tab, as shown below. Make sure the “Attack type” is set to “Cluster bomb”. You'll see the two highlight positions on green.




d) Go to “Payloads” tab and click the Payload set drop down menu. You can see that there are 2 payloads: one for the username and one for the password.

Choose 1. (For username)
You can add as many usernames as you want to. We're going to add only one: “admin”.

Click on “Add” and type "admin".




Choose 2. (For password)

Click on “Load” and select your dictionary. We're going to use “rockyou-20.txt” wordlist from the SecLists repository.



Click on “Start Attack”.

Have a coffee!

You've managed to get the correct password.
